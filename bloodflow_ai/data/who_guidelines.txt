WHO Blood Donation Guidelines

1. ELIGIBILITY CRITERIA

1.1 Age
- Donors must be between 18 and 65 years old.
- Donors over 65 may be accepted with medical approval.

1.2 Weight
- Donors must weigh at least 50 kg (110 lbs).

1.3 Health Status
- Donors must be in good general health.
- Donors with fever, cough, or infection should not donate.

1.4 Hemoglobin Level
- Minimum hemoglobin: 12.5 g/dL for women, 13.0 g/dL for men.

2. DONATION FREQUENCY

2.1 Whole Blood Donation
- Minimum 56 days (8 weeks) between donations.
- Maximum 4-5 donations per year for women, 5-6 for men.

2.2 Platelet Donation
- Minimum 7 days between donations.
- Maximum 24 times per year.

2.3 Plasma Donation
- Minimum 28 days between donations.

3. MEDICATION RESTRICTIONS

3.1 Antibiotics
- Wait 24 hours after the last dose of antibiotics.
- Wait 7 days after completion of treatment for serious infections.

3.2 Aspirin
- Wait 24 hours after last dose for platelet donation.

3.3 Blood Thinners
- Wait 7 days after last dose of anticoagulants.

4. RECENT MEDICAL HISTORY

4.1 Surgery
- Minor surgery: Wait 1 week.
- Major surgery: Wait 6 months.

4.2 Blood Transfusion
- Wait 12 months after receiving a blood transfusion.

4.3 Tattoos and Piercings
- Wait 12 months if done in non-regulated facilities.

5. PREGNANCY AND BREASTFEEDING

5.1 Pregnancy
- Cannot donate during pregnancy.
- Wait 6 months after delivery.

5.2 Breastfeeding
- Can donate 3 months after delivery.

6. BLOOD TYPES AND COMPATIBILITY

6.1 Universal Donor
- O- is the universal donor (can donate to all blood types).

6.2 Universal Recipient
- AB+ is the universal recipient (can receive from all blood types).

6.3 Compatibility Rules
- O- can donate to: all blood types
- O+ can donate to: O+, A+, B+, AB+
- A- can donate to: A-, A+, AB-, AB+
- A+ can donate to: A+, AB+
- B- can donate to: B-, B+, AB-, AB+
- B+ can donate to: B+, AB+
- AB- can donate to: AB-, AB+
- AB+ can donate to: AB+

7. BANGLADESH SPECIFIC GUIDELINES

7.1 National Blood Transfusion Service
- Bangladesh follows WHO guidelines with local adaptations.
- Donors must be registered with the National Blood Transfusion Service.

7.2 Local Requirements
- Valid national ID required for registration.
- Hepatitis B and C screening mandatory.
- HIV screening mandatory.
- Malaria screening in endemic areas.

8. FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

8.1 Can I donate after taking antibiotics?
- Yes, wait 24 hours after the last dose for minor infections.
- Wait 7 days after completion of treatment for serious infections.

8.2 Can I donate if I have high blood pressure?
- Yes, if blood pressure is controlled and within normal limits.

8.3 Can I donate if I have diabetes?
- Yes, if diabetes is well-controlled with diet or medication.

8.4 Can I donate if I have a tattoo?
- Yes, if the tattoo was done in a regulated facility and at least 12 months ago.

8.5 Can I donate if I am pregnant?
- No, you cannot donate during pregnancy.

8.6 How often can I donate blood?
- Every 56 days (8 weeks) for whole blood donation.
- Up to 4-5 times per year for women, 5-6 for men.
